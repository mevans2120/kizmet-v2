Concept 1

Content fades into the background at the bottom edge, implying continuation without any explicit indicator.

How it works

  • CSS gradient overlay from transparent to background color
  • Content is deliberately "cut off" mid-paragraph
  • No interactive elements or animations needed
  • Works on any content length
Strengths
  • Most subtle option
  • Zero distraction
  • Works universally
  • No JS required
Tradeoffs
  • Less discoverable
  • Could seem like rendering bug
  • Passive, not active cue
Concept 2

Breathing Line

A subtle vertical line with gentle "breathing" animation. Spa-like, meditative, and aligned with wellness brand.

Healing Hands, Open Heart

I grew up watching my grandmother's hands work magic on aching muscles and tired spirits. Now I'm carrying that tradition forward, blending generations of natural healing wisdom with modern therapeutic techniques.

How it works

  • Single 1px vertical line, center-aligned
  • Slow "breathing" animation (3s cycle)
  • Line expands/contracts with opacity change
  • Small dot at bottom adds subtle endpoint
Strengths
  • On-brand (calming, spa-like)
  • More discoverable than fade
  • Feels intentional, not error
  • Gentle, not demanding
Tradeoffs
  • Still uses animation
  • Takes visual space
  • Could auto-hide after first scroll
Concept 3

Peek Content

Show the top of the next section, clearly cut off. The visible content itself becomes the scroll indicator.

Third-Generation Healer

Healing has always been the family business. My grandmother was the person everyone in our community called when their backs went out or their...

How it works

  • Hero section doesn't fill viewport
  • Next section's header is visible above fold
  • Content cut off mid-sentence implies continuation
  • Clear section break (color/border) signals new content
Strengths
  • Content IS the indicator
  • No extra UI elements
  • Teases what's coming
  • Most natural pattern
Tradeoffs
  • Requires careful layout sizing
  • Different on every viewport
  • Hero feels less "full bleed"

Recommendation

Fade Edge

Best for: Pages with long-form content where the cut-off text naturally implies more. Simple to implement, but most passive.

Recommended

Peek Content

Best for: Kizmet's About page. The hero doesn't need to be full-viewport. Showing the "My Roots" section header creates natural curiosity and scroll motivation with zero additional UI.

Breathing Line

Best for: When you need something more explicit but still want that spa/wellness feel. Could be combined with fade edge if needed.
